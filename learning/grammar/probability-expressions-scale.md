## Meaning

Comparing probability expressions in English from certain to uncertain

## Usage Context

Certainty scale:

definitely / certainly: 95%+ — Almost certain
will (plain prediction): ~85% — Direct assertion
probably: ~70-80% — Reasoned prediction, slightly informal
be likely to: ~65-75% — Objective judgment, more formal
maybe / perhaps: ~40-60% — Uncertain, could go either way
might / could: ~30-50% — Weaker possibility
unlikely to: ~20-30% — Probably not

Key distinctions:
- probably vs be likely to: both ~70-80%, probably is spoken, be likely to is formal
- maybe vs might: maybe starts the sentence; might follows a subject
- definitely vs certainly: nearly identical; definitely is more emphatic in speech

## Anchor Sentence

She will probably be late. / She is likely to be late. / Maybe she will be late. — same situation, different confidence levels.
