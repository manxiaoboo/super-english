# forcibly vs force as adverb before past participle

## Meaning

force cannot be used directly as an adverb to modify a verb or past participle; use the adverb form forcibly instead

## Usage Context

Wrong: has been force modified / force changed / force updated
Right: has been forcibly modified / forcibly overridden / forcibly changed

Rule: adverbs modify verbs and past participles, not nouns or base verbs.
force (noun/verb) → forcibly (adverb)

Exception: hyphenated compound modifiers before a noun are sometimes acceptable in informal/technical writing:
  force-applied styles (adjective modifier before noun)
  force-quit the app
But these are compounds, not adverb + past participle constructions.

Other common adverb pairs to remember:
automatic → automatically (not: automatic updated)
manual → manually (not: manual configured)
force → forcibly (not: force modified)

## Anchor Sentence

The menu bar styles were forcibly overridden by a third-party plugin.
