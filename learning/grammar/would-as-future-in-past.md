# would as future-in-past

## Meaning

would used to express a future action or state as seen from a past point in time; common after past-tense reporting or thinking verbs

## Usage Context

Pattern: past-tense context + would + verb

Common triggers: thought, said, expected, hoped, assumed, was not prepared for, had no idea

Examples:
- I thought it would be easy. (当时以为会很简单)
- I was not prepared for how hard it would be. (没料到会那么难)
- She said she would finish by Friday. (她说她周五前会完成)
- Nobody expected the migration would take three weeks.

Contrast with present context:
- I am not prepared for how hard it is. (现在正在经历 → is)
- I was not prepared for how hard it would be. (回顾过去的预期 → would be)

## Anchor Sentence

I had no idea the refactoring would take this long.

## Review Questions

1. I thought it would be easy 里的 would 表示什么时间关系？
2. 把这句话改成现在视角：I was not prepared for how hard it would be → I am not prepared for how hard it ___.
3. 用 I never expected ... would ... 造一句和项目或学习有关的句子。
