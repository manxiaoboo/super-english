## Meaning

A wh-clause using how + adjective/adverb + subject + verb to express degree; acts as a noun and can follow prepositions (for, at, about) or verbs (know, realize, imagine, understand)

## Usage Context

Pattern: [preposition / verb] + how + adj/adv + subject + verb

After prepositions:
- I was not prepared for how difficult it would be.
- I was surprised at how quickly he learned.
- Nobody warned me about how stressful onboarding would be.

After verbs:
- I had no idea how hard it would be.
- You will not believe how long this took.
- I now understand how complex the system is.

Note: word order is statement order (S + V), NOT question order.
  Wrong: I realized how difficult was the task.
  Right:  I realized how difficult the task was.

## Anchor Sentence

I was not mentally prepared for how difficult the code review would be.

## Review Questions
